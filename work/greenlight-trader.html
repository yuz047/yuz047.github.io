Method

A daily allocation process with visible guardrails.

Each market day is treated as a new allocation decision. The model reviews trend, relative strength, volatility, pullback quality, breakout behavior, forecast upside, valuation health, and current market stress. It can hold SPY as the baseline, add QQQ or semiconductor beta when conditions support risk, select smaller single-stock sleeves, rotate toward SHY in defensive regimes, or leave buying power in cash.

Risk controls

Designed around SPY-relative performance.

The strategy is measured against SPY rather than against cash alone. The goal is to outperform the benchmark while keeping relative drawdown within a defined budget. Single-stock exposure is deliberately small, cash is allowed, and defensive allocations can replace growth exposure when volatility or trend conditions deteriorate.
