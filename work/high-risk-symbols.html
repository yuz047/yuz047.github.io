Pump-&-Dump Radar · small-cap manipulation surveillance

Two views on small-cap manipulation risk,
refreshed every weekday after the US close.

A daily scan of major-exchange (no-OTC) US-listed micro-caps that fit the classic pump-and-dump profile. An editable rule set (four criteria, all must hold by default) runs alongside a PCA(3) structural model anchored on documented DOJ/SEC cases — it learns the size/liquidity axis those names sit on and flags everything past a percentile cut. The reported list is the union of the two, and every threshold below is tunable. Surveillance tooling, not investment advice.

Method & limits

The editable rule set flags a name when all four default size/liquidity criteria hold for the day, with the "last 20 business days" tests evaluated against the 20-day extreme (worst case). The underwriter watchlist is context and filter metadata, not a counted rule flag. Issuer location is deliberately not a criterion — documented US-listed pump-and-dumps are frequently US issuers. The PCA model is unsupervised on the four size/liquidity features; the anchors only orient the risk axis, and the percentile cut sets how aggressive the flag is. MAG7 and blue-chip points are projected in as visual rulers — they are never scanned, counted, or flagged.

The underwriter watchlist is sourced from FINRA/SEC small-cap "ramp-and-dump" enforcement; the per-symbol underwriter map is a maintained seed — reconcile against syndicate / EDGAR 424B4 records. OTC names are excluded entirely. Not investment advice.
