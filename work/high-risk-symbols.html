High-Risk Symbols · pump-&-dump surveillance

Two views on small-cap manipulation risk,
refreshed every weekday after the US close.

A daily scan that flags US-listed micro-caps fitting the classic pump-and-dump profile. A strict rule-based gate (six criteria, all must hold) runs alongside a PCA(3) structural model that learns the size/liquidity axis the known high-risk names sit on and flags everything past a percentile cut. The reported list is the union of the two — tagged so you can see which view caught each name. Surveillance tooling, not investment advice.

Method & limits

The rule gate encodes the legacy logic verbatim: a symbol is rule-based high-risk only when all six criteria hold for the day, with the "last 20 business days" tests evaluated against the 20-day extreme (worst-case). The PCA model is unsupervised on the four size/liquidity features; the anchors only orient the risk axis (which direction is risky), and the percentile slider sets how aggressive the cut is — the anchors' positions are marked so the cut can be calibrated. The combined list is the union, so a name caught by either view is reported.

The IPO-underwriter flag is the one input no price feed carries; it comes from a maintained map of the nine boutiques' deals and should be reconciled against syndicate / EDGAR 424B4 records. Not investment advice.
